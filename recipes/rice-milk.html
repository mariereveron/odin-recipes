Venezuelan chicha or Rice cinnamon milk is made with rice, water, milk, cinnamon, and sugar. It's a sweet and creamy beverage with a texture similar to a milkshake or smoothie. It's typically served cold and garnished with ground cinnamon on top.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup white rice
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 cloves
  • 1 can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cup evaporated milk or rice cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Ground cinnamon for serving

Steps:

  1. Rinse the rice and add it to a large pot with the water, cinnamon stick, and cloves.
  2. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 45 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the rice is soft and has absorbed most of the water.
  3. Remove the cinnamon stick and cloves from the pot and discard.
  4. Use an immersion blender or transfer the rice mixture to a blender and blend until smooth
  5. Add the sweetened consensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla extract to the blender and blend until everything is well combined.
  6. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh strainer or cheesecloth to remove any remaining bits of rice.
  7. Chill the chicha in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours or until completely chilled.
  8. Serve the chicha cold with a sprinkle of ground cinnamon on top.

Enjoy your delicious Venezuelan chicha with milk and rice!
