Dulce de leche is a creamy caramel-like sauce made from sweetened condensed milk and sugar, popular in Latin American countries.
Particularly popular in Argentina, where it is often used as a spread on toast or as a filling for cakes and pastries.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of sweetened condensed milk (14 oz or 396 g)

Steps:

  1. Fill a pot with water, making sure that the water level is at least 1 inch (2.5 cm) above the can.
  2. Bring the water to a boil and then reduce the heat to low. Let the can simmer for 2-3 hours, making sure to add more water to the pot as needed to keep the can fully submerged.
  3. After 2-3 hours, turn off the heat and carefully remove the can from the pot using tongs or a slotted spoon. Let the can cool to room temperature before opening it.
  4. Once the can has cooled, open it and you'll find thick, creamy dulce de leche inside!

Note: Be very careful when working with the can of condensed milk, as it can become extremely hot and can potentially explode if not fully submerged in water.
Always make sure to check the water level in the pot and add more water as needed to keep the can fully submerged.
